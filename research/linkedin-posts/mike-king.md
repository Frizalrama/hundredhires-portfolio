## Post 3 — ~1 month ago
**Topic:** Cloaking strategy for AI crawlers vs traditional search engines

Cloaking has been a dirty word in SEO for 20+ years. For good reason. If you're showing search engines one thing and users another to manipulate rankings, you're asking to get smoked. But AI crawlers are not search engines. They don't follow the same rules. They don't render like Googlebot. They don't have the same value exchange. And in many cases, they're not sending you meaningful traffic while they're happily consuming the original research, data, and frameworks you spent real money creating. So the question becomes: Why are we treating every AI crawler like it deserves the same access as Google? I'm not saying you should hide everything. That would be stupid. If you want to be cited in AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, or whatever answer engine is next, you need to make the right content accessible. But your moat? Your proprietary benchmarks? Your hard-won datasets? Your internal research? Your original frameworks? That's a different conversation. The web was built around an implicit bargain: You crawl my content. You send me traffic. AI has broken that bargain. So technical SEO needs to evolve from "make everything crawlable" to "make the right things accessible to the right systems for the right reasons." That means thinking at the edge. Thinking about rendering. Thinking about user agents. Thinking about markdown (purely for speed purposes). Thinking about what AI systems can and cannot actually see. And, yes, thinking about cloaking differently.
